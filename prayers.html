Opening Prayer

In the name of the Father, and of the Son, and of the Holy Spirit. Amen.

My Lord, Jesus Christ, You have made this journey to die for me with love unutterable, and I have so many times ungratefully abandoned You. But now I love You with all my heart, and because I love You, I am sincerely sorry for ever having offended You. Pardon me, my God, and permit me to accompany You on this journey. You go to die for love of me; I want, my beloved Redeemer, to die for love of You. My Jesus, I will live and die always united to You. Amen.

Stations of the Cross (Traditional - St. Alphonsus Liguori)

1st Station: Jesus is condemned to death

We adore You, O Christ, and we bless You. Because by Your holy Cross, You have redeemed the world.

Consider how Jesus Christ, after being scourged and crowned with thorns, was unjustly condemned by Pilate to die on the Cross. This sentence filled Him with joy, because it was the hour in which He was to die for the salvation of mankind.

Prayer: My adorable Jesus, it was not Pilate; no, it was my sins that condemned You to die. I beseech You, by the merits of this sorrowful journey, to assist my soul in its journey toward eternity. I love You, my beloved Jesus; I love You more than myself. I repent with my whole heart of having offended You. Never permit me to separate myself from You again.

(Our Father, Hail Mary, Glory Be.)

Our Father
Our Father, who art in heaven, hallowed be Thy name; Thy kingdom come; Thy will be done on earth as it is in heaven. Give us this day our daily bread; and forgive us our trespasses as we forgive those who trespass against us; and lead us not into temptation, but deliver us from evil. Amen.

Hail Mary
Hail Mary, full of grace, the Lord is with thee; blessed art thou among women and blessed is the fruit of thy womb, Jesus. Holy Mary, Mother of God, pray for us sinners, now and at the hour of our death. Amen.

Glory Be
Glory be to the Father, and to the Son, and to the Holy Spirit. As it was in the beginning, is now, and ever shall be, world without end. Amen.

2nd Station: Jesus carries His Cross

We adore You, O Christ, and we bless You. Because by Your holy Cross, You have redeemed the world.

Consider how Jesus, in making this journey with the Cross on His shoulders, thought of us and offered to His Father the death He was about to suffer.

Prayer: My most beloved Jesus, I embrace all the sufferings You have destined for me until death. I beg You, by all You suffered in carrying Your Cross, to give me the help necessary to carry mine with perfect patience and resignation.

(Our Father, Hail Mary, Glory Be.)

3rd Station: Jesus falls the first time

We adore You, O Christ, and we bless You. Because by Your holy Cross, You have redeemed the world.

Consider this first fall of Jesus under His Cross. His flesh was torn by scourges, His head crowned with thorns, and He had lost a great quantity of blood. He was so weakened that He could scarcely walk.

Prayer: My beloved Jesus, it was not the weight of the Cross, but of my sins, which made You suffer so much. By the merits of this first fall, deliver me from the misfortune of falling into mortal sin.

(Our Father, Hail Mary, Glory Be.)

4th Station: Jesus meets His Mother

We adore You, O Christ, and we bless You. Because by Your holy Cross, You have redeemed the world.

Consider the meeting of the Son and the Mother, which took place on this journey. Jesus and Mary looked at each other, and their looks became as so many arrows to wound those hearts which loved each other so tenderly.

Prayer: My most loving Jesus, by the pain You suffered in this meeting, grant me the grace to be truly devoted to Your most holy Mother.

5th Station: Simon helps Jesus carry the Cross

We adore You, O Christ, and we bless You. Because by Your holy Cross, You have redeemed the world.

Consider how the Jews, seeing that Jesus was unable to proceed, forced Simon of Cyrene to carry the Cross behind Our Lord.

Prayer: My sweetest Jesus, I will not refuse the Cross as Simon did: I accept it, I embrace it. I accept in particular the death that is destined for me.

6th Station: Veronica wipes the face of Jesus

We adore You, O Christ, and we bless You. Because by Your holy Cross, You have redeemed the world.

Consider how Veronica, seeing Jesus so afflicted, wiped His face with a cloth, on which was afterwards left the impression of His sacred countenance.

Prayer: My most beloved Jesus, Your face was beautiful before, but in this journey it has lost all its beauty. I offer You my heart; imprint on it Your divine likeness.

7th Station: Jesus falls the second time

We adore You, O Christ, and we bless You. Because by Your holy Cross, You have redeemed the world.

Consider the second fall of Jesus under the Cross, which renews the pain of all the wounds of the head and members of Our afflicted Lord.

Prayer: My most gentle Jesus, how many times have I offended You! By the merits of this new fall, give me the necessary help to persevere in Your grace.

8th Station: Jesus meets the women of Jerusalem

We adore You, O Christ, and we bless You. Because by Your holy Cross, You have redeemed the world.

Consider how these women wept with compassion at seeing Jesus so badly treated. But Jesus said to them: "Weep not for Me, but for yourselves and for your children."

Prayer: My Jesus, laden with sorrows, I weep for the offences I have committed against You. Grant that I may weep for them with a sincere and lasting sorrow.

9th Station: Jesus falls the third time

We adore You, O Christ, and we bless You. Because by Your holy Cross, You have redeemed the world.

Consider the third fall of Jesus Christ. His weakness was extreme and the cruelty of His executioners excessive.

Prayer: My outraged Jesus, by the weakness You suffered in going to Calvary, give me strength enough to conquer all human respect.

Prayer: My innocent Jesus, by the torment You suffered in being stripped of Your garments, help me to strip myself of all attachment to earthly things.

Prayer: My Jesus, loaded with contempt, nail my heart to the Cross, that it may always remain there to love You.

12th Station: Jesus dies on the Cross

We adore You, O Christ, and we bless You. Because by Your holy Cross, You have redeemed the world.

Consider how Jesus, after three hours of agony on the Cross, at length consumed with suffering, abandoned Himself into the hands of His Father and expired.

Prayer: My dying Jesus, I devoutly kiss the Cross on which You died for love of me. I deserve, because of my sins, to die a miserable death; but Your death is my hope.

13th Station: Jesus is taken down from the Cross

We adore You, O Christ, and we bless You. Because by Your holy Cross, You have redeemed the world.

Consider how, after Our Lord had expired, two of His disciples took Him down from the Cross and placed Him in the arms of His afflicted Mother.

Prayer: O Mother of Sorrows, for the love of your Son, accept me as your servant and pray to Him for me.

14th Station: Jesus is laid in the tomb

We adore You, O Christ, and we bless You. Because by Your holy Cross, You have redeemed the world.

Consider how the disciples carried the body of Jesus to the tomb and placed it therein.

Prayer: My buried Jesus, I kiss the stone that encloses You. But You gloriously rose again; I beg You to grant me the grace to rise again with You in glory.

Closing Prayer

Lord Jesus Christ, Your Passion and death have brought life to the world. May we always remember Your sacrifice and live in Your love. Help us to carry our crosses faithfully and follow You always. Amen.
